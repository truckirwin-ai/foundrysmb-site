A · About

A small practice, built for operators who need results.

Foundry exists because most AI advice is too vague, too tool-obsessed, and too far from the workflows that make or lose money.

A.1 · Why this exists

Generic AI advice is a tax on small businesses.

Founders are tired of consultants who have never carried quota, agencies that price like they are building enterprise software, and frameworks that assume a thousand-person workforce.

We translate enterprise-grade operational thinking into something a five to fifty person team can actually run. The sprint is intentionally concrete: diagnose one workflow, build the first AI-assisted version, hand off the operating package, then decide whether the larger install is worth it.

A.2 · The team

Operators with the receipts.

Foundry is led by Truck Irwin, a thirty-year operator who has lived inside enterprise systems, small business pressure, product builds, sales motions, and AI-assisted execution.

The operating pattern is simple: find the workflow with money attached, use agent-assisted work to move fast, build the first version, and turn what works into reusable playbooks, templates, and products.

The proof point: this is not theory. Foundry was built from real operating experience, hands-on product work, and a practical ability to convert messy business context into working systems.

Truck Irwin Founder & Principal · Colorado

A.3 · How we operate

Three commitments. Held tight.

  1. Senior operator judgment

    Every engagement is led by someone who understands delivery pressure, business tradeoffs, and the difference between interesting and useful.

  2. Cash-first scope

    The primary offer starts at $1,500 because the first workflow should be narrow enough to buy quickly and valuable enough to matter.

  3. Systems you can own

    You leave with the map, prompts, SOPs, context structure, and handoff assets needed to operate the workflow without mystery.
