# Foundry Toolkit

Free operating assets for business owners who need AI to improve real work, not create another pile of experiments.

The Toolkit is not a prompt library. It is a set of business operating guides for finding one workflow, understanding its economics, building the first AI-assisted version, and deciding whether the work should become a sprint, install, or managed operating system.

## The three free toolkits

| Toolkit | Best for | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [AI Revenue Sprint Kit](ai-revenue-sprint-kit.md) | Owners who know AI might help but do not know where to start | Choose the workflow most likely to pay and define the first sprint target. |
| [Contractor Bid Automation Starter Pack](contractor-bid-automation-starter-pack.md) | Contractors, trades, local services, estimators, and owner-led quoting teams | Turn scattered lead notes into a bid-prep packet, clarification email, and follow-up path. |
| [Context Vault Builder](context-vault-builder.md) | Knowledge-heavy teams with facts, rules, examples, and SOPs scattered everywhere | Build the source-of-truth structure that makes AI outputs more accurate and reviewable. |

## How to use the toolkit

1. Start with the AI Revenue Sprint Kit.
2. Score three workflows.
3. Pick one workflow with cost, repetition, data/examples, and decision authority.
4. If the workflow is bid/estimate related, use the Contractor Bid Automation Starter Pack.
5. If the workflow depends on company knowledge, build the Context Vault first.
6. Run the QA checklist before letting any AI output reach a customer.
7. If the workflow has visible economics, book the sprint or move into an install.

## What makes this different

Most beginner AI advice says: "try prompts." That is backwards.

Foundry starts with:

- the workflow owner
- the business outcome
- the failure points
- the data sources
- the review gates
- the operating procedure
- the economics

AI is useful only when it is inside that structure.
