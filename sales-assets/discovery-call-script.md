# AI Revenue Sprint Discovery Call Script

## Goal

Decide whether one painful workflow is worth a $1,500 five-day sprint and whether a larger install could follow.

## Opening

"Thanks for taking the time. I am not here to sell generic AI. I want to understand one workflow that is wasting time, slowing revenue, or making your team repeat itself. If it is a fit, the sprint is $1,500 and we build a working prototype in five business days."

## Qualification questions

1. What workflow made you fill out the form?
2. What triggers that workflow?
3. Who touches it before it is done?
4. What tools or documents does it pass through?
5. Where does it slow down, break, or depend on memory?
6. How often does this happen each week or month?
7. What happens financially when it goes badly?
8. What would make a five-day sprint feel obviously worth it?
9. Who has authority to approve access, payment, and workflow changes?
10. Are you able to provide real examples before kickoff?

## Fit scoring

- Pain is specific: 0-3
- Workflow repeats often: 0-3
- Financial impact is visible: 0-3
- Buyer has authority: 0-3
- Examples are available: 0-3
- Client can review during sprint: 0-3

**Strong fit:** 14-18  
**Maybe:** 9-13  
**No fit:** 0-8

## Close for sprint

"This looks like a fit because [specific reasons]. The next step is the $1,500 sprint. I will send the kickoff checklist, collect examples, and we will start with one workflow target: [target]."

## Close for not now

"The pain is real, but I do not think the sprint should start until [missing condition] is solved. I recommend [specific homework] and a follow-up on [date]."

## Close for no fit

"I do not want to take your money for a sprint that will not produce a useful result. The issue seems to be [reason]. Here is the better next move: [recommendation]."
