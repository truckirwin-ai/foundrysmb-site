# Founder-Led Sales Sprint Pack

Ten working days to create qualified workflow conversations and sell the first paid AI Revenue Sprints.

## Target

Book:

- 10 qualified discovery calls
- 3 paid AI Revenue Sprints
- 1 install or managed ops opportunity

## Sprint philosophy

The goal is not to create "AI awareness." The goal is to create conversations with people who can buy because one workflow is costing them time, money, speed, quality, or owner attention.

Every touch should do one of three things:

- name a likely workflow leak
- offer a useful diagnostic asset
- invite a narrow decision about whether the workflow is worth fixing

Do not sell AI. Sell recovered owner time, faster follow-up, cleaner bids, better reporting, fewer rework loops, and operating discipline.

## Daily minimum

- 20 researched prospects
- 10 first-touch messages
- 5 follow-ups
- 2 warm-network asks
- 1 public workflow teardown post
- 1 CRM cleanup block
- 1 message review: what got replies, what sounded soft, what needs sharpening

## Best first prospects

Look for owner-led businesses with visible workflow pain:

- contractors and trades with quote forms
- local service companies with slow follow-up
- professional service firms with reporting burden
- training/enablement businesses with content operations
- managed service providers with ticket/report workflows
- consultants with proposal and client-update drag

## ICP filters

Prioritize prospects with:

- 5-75 employees
- owner, GM, operator, or practice leader visible online
- high-value work where delays matter
- repeated proposals, reports, estimates, tickets, onboarding, or follow-ups
- messy intake forms, PDFs, spreadsheets, or email-heavy operations
- enough margin to pay for a $1,500 sprint
- enough recurring workflow pain to become install or managed ops

Avoid prospects where:

- the business is too tiny to pay
- the workflow is highly regulated and you lack domain support
- the owner wants a chatbot before naming a workflow
- the value depends on replacing human judgment
- there is no access to examples

## Lead list build rules

Build prospects in batches of 25.

For each lead, capture:

- company
- website
- owner/operator
- LinkedIn or email source
- industry
- likely workflow
- visible evidence
- first message angle
- lead score
- next action date
- status

Batch quality test:

- 20 of 25 should have a named workflow angle.
- 15 of 25 should have visible evidence.
- 10 of 25 should be plausible $1,500 buyers.
- 5 of 25 should be strong enough for a personalized note.

## Discovery call agenda

30 minutes:

1. Confirm business and workflow.
2. Ask what happens when the workflow fails.
3. Identify examples and current tools.
4. Separate judgment work from repeatable support work.
5. Define first version.
6. Confirm owner/review availability.
7. Decide: no fit, free toolkit, sprint, install, managed ops.

## Call control questions

Use these to keep calls from becoming abstract AI talk.

- What workflow made you take this call?
- What happens when it fails?
- How often does it happen?
- Who owns it today?
- What does the owner still have to do manually?
- What examples exist?
- What decisions should AI never make?
- What output would be useful enough to test in five business days?
- What would make this worth $1,500?
- If the first version works, what would you want installed next?

## Objection handling

### "We already use ChatGPT."

Good. The question is whether it is inside a workflow with source context, review gates, and measurable output. Most usage is still individual experimentation.

### "We need a full platform."

Maybe later. First prove one workflow. Platforms bought before process clarity usually become another tool nobody owns.

### "Can AI do this accurately?"

Only with the right structure: source context, constrained tasks, examples, and human review. Foundry does not remove judgment from risky work.

### "Why pay for a sprint?"

Because the cost is not the prompt. The cost is diagnosing the workflow, building the first version, testing examples, documenting the SOP, and deciding whether the system deserves installation.

### "Can you guarantee revenue?"

No. The sprint does not guarantee revenue. It reduces uncertainty around one workflow and creates a working first version. We measure whether that workflow can save time, improve speed, recover follow-up, reduce rework, or support revenue.

### "Can we start with strategy?"

Only if strategy means choosing the first workflow. Broad AI strategy without a workflow usually becomes theater.

### "Can you just send prompts?"

Yes, the free Toolkit includes prompts. The paid sprint is for diagnosis, context, examples, review gates, and a working first version tied to your business.

## Proposal trigger

Send a proposal only when these are true:

- one workflow is named
- business pain is clear
- buyer or strong sponsor is present
- examples are available
- review owner can participate
- sprint outcome can be described in one sentence
- $1,500 is not a surprise

Proposal sentence:

```text
In five business days, Foundry SMB will turn [workflow] into a reviewed first-version operating asset that helps [role] produce [output] faster and more consistently, using your real examples and a human approval gate.
```
